Code till dead - A journey of self-development

Hello there! My name is Fabio, otherwise known as the aeolicdino. I'm starting on a journey of learning and advancement of my skills mostly because it scares me how fast I am aging and I fear not being able to accomplish my dreams.

Code till dead is a challenge in discipline and focus. Recently I have been thinking deeply about how despite me spending considerable time studying computer science and coding, I still remain way behind my peers in terms of programming skill. Part of the blame lies in the rocky teenage and early adult years that I lived. But more so, I believe that not being able to focus deeply on a single activity has negatively impacted my ability to adquire any long-lasting knowledge. I have come to the conclusion that spreading yourself too thin is not an effective strategy when it comes to becoming good at something -- A conversation for another time perhaps.

Today is my first day writing this journal. I expect not many people will arrive here, but I feel the need to explain the motivations for the project nonetheless. This blog will serve indirectly as a record of my progress as I develop my skills. Hopefully, as I become a better developer and writer, the quality of my blog will improve as well. From the quality and content of my writing, to the projects featured in my blogs, I suppose everything will become better in time.

To those who read, you do not have to agree with anything I write about. In fact, I presume many of my own personal convictions fall outside what is considred "acceptable" in modern social norm. Remember that as much as I am grateful for it, you were the one who ultimately chose to read on. At any time if you find yourself overly emotional over a topic written in here, take a chill pill and spend some time somewhere else.

That being said, I welcome criticism! We shall abide from now on by a single principle when offering our opinions - prejudice egalitarianism. You are free to share your raw unfiltered opinions even if offensive. I just ask that you remain consistent in your arguments and try whenever possible to remember that no one is perfect. Having agreed on the premise that no one is perfect, let us try to criticize everything in a similar manner. If you think Java is shit, remember that JavaScript is worse.

That would be all for today! Good luck to me, and see you next time!
